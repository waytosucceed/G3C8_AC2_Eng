Understanding Diphthongs and Digraphs


Overview of Grammar topics:

Diphthongs are vowel sounds formed by gliding from one vowel sound to another within the same syllable.
Example: "oi" in "coin," "ou" in "house."

Digraphs are pairs of letters that together represent a single sound.
Example: "sh" in "ship," "ch" in "chair."

Vowel digraphs are pairs of vowels that together represent a single vowel sound.
Example: "ea" in "beach," "ee" in "see."

Consonant digraphs are pairs of consonants that together represent a single consonant sound.
Example: "ch" in "chair," "th" in "thin."

Let us now take three words from our story and learn about their meanings:

Snuggled: To settle or move into a warm, comfortable, and cosy position, often with someone or something.
Example: "He snuggled down into his bed of hay."

Drifted: To move slowly, gently, or smoothly from one place, position, or condition to another.
Example: "He drifted off to sleep."

Content: Feeling happy and satisfied with one's current situation, state of being, or circumstances.
Example: “I am feeling happy and content.”
